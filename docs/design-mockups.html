Visual design · v1 · handoff spec

GhostRace

MARIO KART FOR THE REAL WORLD

iOS 17+ · SWIFTUI

DARK-ONLY BY DESIGN

The identity lives where the product does: on the road, after dark, against a friend. You are blaze orange — hi-vis, the color runners actually wear. The ghost is spectral ice — an outline, never solid. Everything sits on asphalt, and every number is set in mono like a stopwatch.

01 · The calls

Five open questions, answered

Q1 · Brand mark

The checkered ghost

A ghost outline whose wavy hem carries the finish-flag checker. Wordmark is heavy oblique caps — "Ghost" solid, "Race" as an outline, the ghost of the word itself.

Q2 · Racer identity

Shape before color

You = a blaze chevron (a direction, a vehicle). Opponent = the ghost glyph in ice with a dashed trail. Distinguishable in grayscale — colorblind-safe by form, not hue.

Q3 · Segment detail

Route silhouette hero — yes, in v1

The polyline already exists in the data. Drawn as a single ice stroke on asphalt it makes every segment feel like a named circuit, not a database row. No map tiles needed.

Q4 · How far toward "game"

Arcade at the moments, athletic at rest

Full game energy is spent on exactly three beats: countdown, lead change, finish. Everything between is a legible sports instrument. No ambient particles, no screen shake while racing.

Q5 · Light mode?

Dark-only, committed

The race flow already forces dark; splitting the app into two worlds halves the identity. One night-race world, tuned for max-brightness sunlight legibility where it counts (the HUD).

04 · Signature component

TrackBar anatomy

The Mario Kart strip. Every rule here exists so it reads in one glance at 12 km/h.

Alex
You
start gate — thin chalk tick, no label needed
ghost rides below the line with a dashed ice trail — where it's been, not just where it is
finish is always a checker block, pinned; dots clamp 16 px inside both ends

Motion: dots ease 0.8 s per tick (matches GPS cadence). On a lead change the chevron passes through the ghost with a 200 ms ice shimmer — the one moment the strip is allowed to show off.

06 · Motion & sound moments

Where the game lives

Countdown
Blaze ring drains per second, numeral swaps with a hard cut (no crossfade). GO = full-screen green flash, 120 ms, once. Tone + haptic tick per number.
Lead change
White banner skews in ("YOU TOOK THE LEAD" / "ALEX TOOK THE LEAD"), field crossfades green↔red over 600 ms, chevron shimmers through the ghost on the TrackBar. Jingle or warning tone + distinct haptic.
Final stretch
No new visuals — audio cadence escalates (already in RaceCueScheduler). The footnote line switches to "final stretch". Restraint here makes the finish land.
Finish
Checker strip wipes down over the HUD like tape at the line, then the result screen settles. Victory taunt / defeat sting.
Everything else
0.8 s ease on TrackBar dots, standard sheet transitions, nothing ambient. Respect Reduce Motion: cuts instead of wipes, no shimmer.

Handoff

Implementation targets ios/GhostRace/ (SwiftUI, iOS 17+). Tokens → a Theme.swift + asset catalog; display face = SF Pro Black Italic via .system().weight(.black).italic(); instruments = SF Mono via .monospaced(). All states, data, and copy per docs/design-brief.md — this page decides only how it looks. Accessibility contract: ahead/behind never rely on hue alone (sign + wordmark + dot order); Dynamic Type scales all pre/post-race screens; HUD caps at accessibility sizes.
